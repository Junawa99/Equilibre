Justification








Wireframe-About Page


We decided that apart from the landing page wireframe, we will make one about the about page since the about page presents important information such as the values of our brand and it introduces our team.







Wireframe-Home Page


The Home page is the first thing the customers see when visiting our website. This is the reason we put a video which speaks nature, peace and balance and we want people to see Equilibre as these 3 things.






Color palette

We are using the same color codes as the ones in our Brand Road Map. We wanted to use natural and earth colors, because we want our website to have a minimalistic style. Our brand speaks earth, balance, nature, peace and we connect usually these with colors such as brown, beige, green, white.







Choice of Fonts:

We are using the same fonts that we used in the Brand Road Map. For the Headers we are using Baskerville as a font, because it speaks elegance to us and is also the font that the skin-care company Lancome uses for their logo, which was an inspiration for us.


We used Times New Roman as out text font, because it is a soft font and is commonly used in magazines and skin-care websites. We also wanted the headings font and the text font to match and suit each other.








Flow Diagram


The Flow Diagram shows the taskflow of our website. We decided the style of the website to be minimalistic and simple. We have a navbar with a home, about, mission, product, justiffication page. We also have a contact form that can be found in the about page. From the product page people can shop and go to checkout. For the layout and structure of the website we took some ideas and inspiration from the website of N.A.E, because we personally like their brand and what they offer, which is also skin-care products.





User testing reports

We did our testing with 15 people. We first showed the people 2 pages of the paper prototype of the website-the home page and the about page. We did that by creating a survey, putting the pages there and asking the people if they think the website will look visually appealing and if the layout is likable and simple, because this was our goal. All of the 15 people find the paper prototypes likable and said thatit has a clear and simple style that fits our brand's image.
After we were done with the website we showed it to the same 15 people by again creating a survey and asking them 6 questions:
1. Do they find the content of the pages interesting and reletaing to the brand.
2. Are the visuals appealing and matching with the aesthetic of the website.
3. Is the website easy to navigate.
4. What they think of the position of the different elements of the website and the layout.
5. Are there any useability issues they found.
6.What kind recommendations of improvment they would do.

The outcome of the user testing is that there were some issues with the responity of the website if you open it via a mobile phone, but we fixed that. The recommendations we got are that the layout, the structure, the visuals were very liked, but some of the participants said that we should elaborate more on the content, because some of the information wasn't enough such as the the one in the home page. People said that we should include more information about the ingredients of our products and some more visuals, not only the video.
